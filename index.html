Marc "Tryndamere" Merrill

Riot games's co-founder.

Marc Merrill's career

Marc Merril is born on August 17 of 1980 in California.
He attended the University of Southern California from 1998 to 2002 and worked as an analyst at U.S. Bank.
Then, from 2004 to 2006, Marc worked as a Corporate marketing manager at Advanstar Communications.
Finally, he founded Riot Games in 2006 with Brandon Beck.

Brief Riot Games's history

Riot Games was founded as an indie game developer in 2006 by Brandon "Ryze" Beck and Marc "Tryndamere" Merrill in Los Angeles.
The company announced its only game, "League of Legends: Clash of Fates", in October 2008, and released the game in October 2009 as simply "League of Legends".
Their game uses a free-to-play model, supported by microtransactions rather than ads or boxed copy sales.

In 2008, Riot Games obtained initial funding of US$7 million provided by venture capital firms Benchmark Capital and FirstMark Capital.
In a second round of funding in 2009, the company raised $8 million from Benchmark, FirstMark, and Chinese technology company Tencent Holdings.
In early 2011, Tencent Holdings bought out a majority stake in Riot Games.
Tencent later reported the deal was for $231,465,000 in an interim report.
On November 8, 2013, Riot Games announced that the company would relocate to a new building in West Los Angeles in 2015.
On December 16, 2015, Riot Games sold its remaining equity to Tencent Holdings.
On March 8, 2016, Riot announced its acquisition of Radiant Entertainment.
